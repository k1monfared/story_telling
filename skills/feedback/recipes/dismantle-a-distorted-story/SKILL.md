## When to reach for this

You received feedback and your reaction is noticeably stronger than the content seems to warrant. Or you realize that your story about what the feedback means has ballooned: what started as "you run late" is now "I am a bad doctor who doesn't care about patients." You cannot productively engage with what the feedback says or decide whether to act on it until you can see it at actual size. Reach for this before deciding whether to accept, reject, or act on any feedback that has triggered a strong reaction.

## The move

**1. Map your wiring profile.** Apply `understand-your-wiring-profile`. Before engaging with the content of the feedback, name your baseline (high or low), swing (narrow or wide), and sustain/recovery (fast or slow). This step is about attributing your emotional reaction correctly: some fraction of its intensity belongs to the feedback's content; some fraction belongs to your nervous system's built-in configuration. Naming the profile separates "how I experience this kind of feedback" from "how bad the feedback actually is."

Step 1 must come first because without it, steps 2-4 will be applied from inside a distorted emotional state with no recognition that the state is partly wiring-driven. A slow-recovery person who does not know they are a slow-recovery person will mistake their extended distress for evidence that the feedback is catastrophic, and the containment work of steps 3-4 will be undermined from the start.

**2. Name the distortion type.** Apply `notice-the-distortion-type`. Now that you know the reaction is partly wiring, identify which of the three distortion patterns is running: Google bias (past is being flooded with confirming failures), flooding (one specific feedback overflows into all-or-nothing present self-image), or forever bias and snowballing (current feeling projected as permanent or as the first domino in a cascade). Write down the pattern in one sentence: "I'm Google-biasing" or "I'm flooding the scope" or "I'm snowballing into the future."

Step 2 must come before steps 3-4 because the distortion type names what is happening before you try to do anything about it. You cannot pull apart strands you have not identified, and you cannot contain a story whose shape you have not named.

**3. Separate feeling, story, and feedback.** Apply `separate-feeling-story-feedback`. Ask and answer three questions in sequence, writing each answer before moving to the next: (a) What do I feel? (name the emotion and describe it physically.) (b) What is the story I am telling, and inside the story, what is the threat? (listen to it without judging it.) (c) What was the actual feedback? (the exact words, without the story added.) If the reaction connects to older material (last-straw dynamic, open wound), name that too: "this is also activating a ten-year history of being told I'm irresponsible."

Step 3 must come before step 4 because containment has to know what to contain. If the actual feedback is still merged with the story and the feeling, the containment work will contain the wrong thing. The chapter makes this explicit: "you have to be clear about what you've added, and be aware of your patterns over time for the kinds of things you tend to add."

**4. Contain the story to actual size.** Apply `contain-the-story`. Now that the strands are separated and the actual feedback is visible, restore the three containment laws the distortion violated. Use whichever of the three tools fits the distortion type identified in step 2: Feedback Containment Chart (what this IS about / what it is NOT about) for flooding and Google-bias patterns; Balancing Picture for flooding when visual proportion is the issue; Right-sizing future consequences for snowballing and forever-bias patterns. For each tool, write your actual answers. The process is not complete until you can state the actual feedback in one sentence that fits inside the left column of the containment chart.

## Chain ordering is intrinsic

Each step depends on the prior:

- Step 1 fails if skipped: the work in steps 2-4 proceeds from inside a distorted emotional state with no recognition that the intensity is partly wiring-driven. Alita who does not know her wiring interprets her devastation as accurate evidence that the feedback is catastrophic and cannot contain a story she believes is accurate.
- Step 2 fails if skipped: you cannot separate strands (step 3) without knowing which distortion type has fused them.
- Step 3 fails if skipped: containment (step 4) operates on the actual feedback, not on the story. Without strand separation, the containment work takes the wrong thing as its input.
- Step 4 fails if skipped: the chain ends with separated strands but no restored scope boundaries. The actual feedback is visible but still at distorted size. You have not yet arrived at "actual size."

## Signal it landed

You can write the actual feedback in one sentence. The sentence fits in the left column of a containment chart. The threat inside your story has been named and distinguished from what the feedback actually said. You can name which wiring tendencies amplified the reaction and which distortion pattern ran. The feedback now feels significantly smaller than it did when you started.

## Probe before & after

Before: ask "if I had to write down exactly what the feedback said in one sentence, could I do it without the sentence expanding into my story about what it means?" If no, at least one of the four steps has not completed.

After: ask "can I name three things separately: what I feel, what my story about the feedback is, and what the feedback actually said?" If yes, step 3 has landed. Then: "does my story stay inside the domain of the feedback, or does it cover my whole past, present, and future?" If it stays inside the domain, step 4 has landed.

## Failure mode this prevents

Without the chain, the distorted story substitutes for the feedback in every evaluation and decision that follows. You either accept the distorted story as accurate (and are devastated by a one-line comment in a patient survey) or reject the distorted story wholesale (and miss the actionable signal it contained). The chapter: "What's so amazing about these distortions is how real they appear to us in the moment... unless we are consciously looking for it, we can't see the gap when we're in it." The chain is the looking.

## Worked example

Seth's case provides all four steps in sequence. His wiring (step 1): sensitive, slow-recovery profile. His distortion type (step 2): forever bias and snowballing — from one offhand boss comment to "I'll never succeed at this job; my personal life is a mess." Strand separation (step 3): Feeling: nausea and dread. Story: "My boss disapproves of me; I'll lose this job; I can't live with myself." Actual feedback: one comment about "everyone" being disorganized, including Seth. Containment (step 4): Left column — whether Seth has room to improve his organizational habits. Right column — whether Seth is fundamentally incompetent, whether his boss disapproves of him as a person, whether his trip to his father's birthday was idiotic. After containment: Seth went, had a wonderful time, and later found his initial reaction incomprehensible. That incomprehensibility is the signal that the chain completed.

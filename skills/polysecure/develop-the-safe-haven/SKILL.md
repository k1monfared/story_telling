## When to reach for this

A partner has come to you with something hard — fear, sadness, news of a hurt — and you notice your impulse is to do something about it: solve, reassure, defend, deflect. Reach for this whenever the partner needs to be held in the difficulty, not pulled out of it.

## The move

1. Slow down. The first move is usually not the right move. Whatever you were about to say, hold it.
2. Acknowledge before responding. "That sounds really hard" or simply "I'm here." Skip if it'd feel performative; the silence and the body language do the work too.
3. Don't try to fix unless they ask. Most safe-haven moments don't want a fix; they want a witness.
4. Don't take it personally if the difficulty is about you. They can be telling you something hurt them and need you to hold it without flinching. The simultaneous practice: hold them AND don't collapse into shame.
5. Stay with them long enough that the difficulty has room to be there. Not five minutes between other agenda items; long enough that it's actually held.
6. Ask what they need. "What would help right now?" The answer might be "just sit with me," might be "tell me it'll be okay," might be "help me think about what to do." Match their actual need.

## Signal it landed

After a hard conversation, the partner says some version of "I feel less alone with this" or simply seems more settled. They reach for you again the next time something is hard. The relationship has the felt quality of "I can come here when I need to."

## Failure mode this prevents

Without safe-haven function, partners learn that bringing hard things to you costs them more energy than it gives. They start managing your reactions instead of bringing the actual difficulty. The relationship becomes a place you can't bring your full weight to.

## Reflection prompts

- How does each partner already act as a safe haven for you?
- In what ways do they show up for you that feel supportive and comforting?
- How could a partner help you feel even more safe with them, in general or in specific situations?
- In what ways can you show up more as a safe haven for each partner?

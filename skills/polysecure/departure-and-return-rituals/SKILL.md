## When to reach for this

You're about to leave for a date with another partner and you can feel both you and your nesting partner tense up — but neither of you has named what would help. Or you just got home and you can feel the ambient awkwardness of "now what?" Reach for this whenever the transitions are doing damage by being unmarked.

## The move

1. Identify what each of you needs at departure. The partner staying home: a specific affirmation, a hug, a reminder that they remain particular to you, a heads-up about expected return time. The partner going: maybe a moment to fully arrive at the door before stepping out, maybe a shared word about how long you'll be gone.
2. Identify what each of you needs at return. The partner returning: time to land, to fully arrive in this relationship's space again, not to be quizzed immediately. The partner who stayed: some kind of reconnection — a hug, a kiss, eye contact, "I missed you" — before logistics.
3. Make it small enough to keep. A fifteen-second hug at the door. A specific phrase you say when leaving ("I'm going, I'll be back, I love you"). A shared moment when you walk back in before phones come out.
4. Don't over-disclose at the return unprompted. The returning partner doesn't owe a play-by-play of the date. The partner who stayed doesn't have to perform interest. What matters is the reconnection, not the report.
5. Adjust as you learn. The ritual that works in month one might not work in month six. The point is the practice, not the specific form.

## Signal it landed

Departures and returns feel like small reliable events, not minor crises. The partner staying home knows what to expect; the partner returning knows how to come back. Both of you can describe the rituals you use without effort.

## Failure mode this prevents

Without departure and return rituals, every transition is a small unmarked rupture. The partner staying home reads the ambient absence as displacement; the partner returning doesn't fully arrive. Over time, the relationship accumulates a quiet wear from these unmarked transitions that no specific event can be blamed for.

## Reflection prompts

- What do you and your partners each need to reconnect with each other after being apart?
- Are there ways of checking in or saying goodbye before going on a date with another person that would create more safety and security?
- How do you want to connect or be approached when you return from time with another partner?
- What rituals have you tried that worked? What have you tried that didn't?

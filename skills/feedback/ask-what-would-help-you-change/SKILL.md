## When to reach for this

You have taken responsibility for your part. You have seen the broader system contributing to the problem. The giver, however, is not ready or able to look at their contribution. They are still stuck in the frame that this feedback exchange is entirely about what you need to change. Asking them directly to acknowledge their role has not worked, and pressing harder will derail the conversation.

## The move

Rather than trying to force the giver to acknowledge and take responsibility for their part in the problem, describe how they could get a better reaction from you. You are asking them to change, but you are framing it, legitimately, as a request that serves your change.

The structure is:
1. Acknowledge your own pattern or reaction, including that you are working on it.
2. Describe specifically what the giver's behavior does to that reaction.
3. Name what they could do differently that would help you do what they are asking.

The chapter's worked script from Gil to Sandy: "I have the strongest reaction to being surprised, because it makes me panic about where else you might be spending and not telling me. I know I'm overreacting sometimes and I'm working on that. It would help me if you would be willing to be upfront with me about the Grape-Nuts and the mochaccino 'mini-vacations,' and we can budget for them together."

That script contains all three components: Gil names his reaction and owns that it is out of proportion (step one); he names what triggers the reaction, the surprise, not the spending itself (step two); he describes a specific change that would help him manage his reaction (step three). The framing is "help me change" rather than "you are doing something wrong."

**The legitimacy condition.** The chapter notes the framing is legitimate: the request is genuinely in service of the receiver's change, not just a tactic. If it is not genuine, meaning if you do not actually believe the giver's behavior contributes to your reaction, this move becomes manipulation. Use it only when the systems observation is honest.

## Signal it landed

The giver has heard a request from you that focuses on something they can do, framed as serving your change. They are no longer on the defensive about their contribution; instead, they are considering a concrete behavioral adjustment. The conversation has moved from "who is to blame" to "what would help."

## Probe before & after

Before: check that you have already taken responsibility for your own part. This move does not replace owning your contribution; it is what you do when, after owning your contribution, the giver still cannot see theirs.

After: ask whether your request was specific and framed as "this helps me change" rather than "this is what you are doing wrong." If it was general or framed as critique, it will have been heard as message two from take-responsibility-first-then-introduce-the-system rather than as a request.

## Failure mode this prevents

Without this move, when a giver is stuck in the "it's all about you" frame, the receiver's options narrow to: accept the feedback and remain silent about the system (full absorber stance), or push the systems point directly and have it heard as deflection. Neither option produces useful change. The "here's what would help me change" framing creates a third path that moves the conversation forward without requiring the giver to abandon their frame. The chapter frames it: "Rather than trying to force them to admit to and take responsibility for their part in the problem, describe how they could get a better reaction from you."

## Worked micro-example

The chapter uses Gil and Sandy as the illustration. Sandy's feedback to Gil is implicit throughout the chapter: stop obsessing, stop confronting, stop making every small purchase a crisis. Gil cannot easily respond "you're right and also your hidden-spending behavior is part of what triggers me," because Sandy will hear that as "so actually it's your fault." What Gil can say is the script above: name his reaction, own it, and ask for something Sandy can give that would help him manage it. The framing shifts the conversation from mutual accusation to a negotiation about what each person can do.

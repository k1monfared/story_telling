## When to reach for this

You realize it's been a while since you said anything specifically affectionate to a partner — not generic ("I love you"), but particular ("the way you raise your eyebrow when you don't believe me makes me happy"). Reach for this whenever the relationship has settled into competent co-management without the visible delight that marked it earlier. In nonmonogamy: reach for this especially before or after a date with another partner, when the partner not on the date most needs to know they remain irreplaceable.

## The move

1. Notice particular things. Not types — particular instances. The specific way they laugh at a particular kind of joke. The way they sit when they're listening hard. The exact phrase they use when they're delighted.
2. Tell them what you noticed. Out loud. With specificity. "I love that you do that thing with your hands when you're explaining something" lands very differently than "you're so smart."
3. Don't confuse delight with reassurance. Reassurance is a response to insecurity ("you're great, don't worry"). Delight is unprompted appreciation ("I keep thinking about how you handled that conversation last week").
4. Calibrate to what they receive well. Some partners want it constantly; some find it overwhelming; many have a specific channel — words, touch, gifts, time, acts — that lands more than the others.
5. Especially before or after a date with another partner, deliberately offer expressed delight. The body of the partner left at home (or returning) needs the explicit signal: you remain unique to me.

## Signal it landed

Partners can recall recent specific things you said about what you love about them. They feel particular to you, not interchangeable with hypothetical other partners. The relationship has texture beyond functioning.

## Failure mode this prevents

Without explicit, particular delight, partners begin to feel like one of several functional connections. Nonmonogamy can accelerate this if delight becomes generic ("I love you" in identical wording to multiple partners). The remedy isn't restricting connection; it's particularizing the delight.

## Reflection prompts

- How do you already show expressed delight for your partners?
- Would your partners like more or different expressed delight from you? How can you provide this?
- How do your partners let you know that you are unique, special, and precious to them?
- Are there moments (before or after a partner is on a date with someone else) when expressed delight is more or less supportive to you?

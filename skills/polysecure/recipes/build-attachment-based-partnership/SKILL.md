## When to reach for this

You want a relationship to become attachment-based (or to become more securely attachment-based) and you want a structured way to do that work rather than hoping it happens. Reach for this whenever you and a partner have agreed to build attachment together and want shared moves to work on.

## The move

1. **Name the relationship type explicitly.** Use `name-the-relationship-type` and `distinguish-attachment-relationships` so both of you know what you're building. Don't skip this — implicit agreements about attachment produce hurt later.
2. **Establish the foundations.** With each partner you want attachment-based connection with, talk through the foundation questions: `develop-the-safe-haven` and `develop-the-secure-base`. Where is each of you already strong? Where would you each like more?
3. **Practice H — Be here.** Use `be-here-with-each-partner` deliberately. Put phones away. Fully arrive when you arrive. Notice when you're absent and bring yourself back. This is the foundation everything else rests on.
4. **Practice E — Express particular delight.** Use `express-particular-delight`. Notice specific things about this person and tell them. Especially: when transitioning between partners or after a date with someone else, deliberately offer expressed delight to the partner not on the date.
5. **Practice A — Attune.** Use `attune-to-your-partner`. Track shifts. Ask. Listen. Don't rush to interpret or fix. Update your model of who they are right now.
6. **Practice R — Rituals.** Use `build-relationship-rituals` and the more specific `departure-and-return-rituals` for nonmonogamous transitions. Pick small reliable practices and keep them.
7. **Practice T — Turn toward after rupture.** Use `turn-toward-after-rupture`. When ruptures happen — and they will — initiate the repair. Don't keep score on whose turn it is.
8. **Don't skip the S — secure self.** Run `become-polysecure-with-self` in parallel. The partner-facing work plateaus quickly without the self-facing work.
9. **Read jealousy as information.** When jealousy fires (and it will), use `read-jealousy-as-information` to find the underlying need rather than acting on the spike.
10. **Watch boundaries.** Pay attention to whether your boundaries skew porous or rigid in this specific relationship and apply `tighten-porous-boundaries` or `soften-rigid-boundaries` as needed.

## Signal it landed

The relationship has the felt sense of attachment without dependence — you can be apart without crisis and together without losing yourself. Both of you know specific moves you're working on; both of you can name what you're getting from each other; ruptures happen but repair too. The body recognizes this person as one of your people.

## Failure mode this prevents

Without a structured practice, attachment-based partnership in nonmonogamy tends to drift toward either over-reliance (the partner becomes the regulator) or under-investment (the relationship coasts on logistics without depth). The HEARTS framework gives both partners specific, namable work to do.

## Reflection prompts

- For each partner you want this with, which HEARTS letter is currently most underdeveloped?
- Which letter is currently strongest? What is it about this relationship that makes that letter work?
- What does success in this relationship look like over six months? Over two years?
- What conversations do you and your partner need to have that you've been avoiding?

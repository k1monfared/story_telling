Brené Brown's *Atlas of the Heart* is not a book of techniques. It is a book of names. Her wager is that the resolution at which we can describe what we feel sets the ceiling on what we can do with it. In place of skills, this volume of Marginalia ships an interactive **feelings selector**: explore the eighty-seven feelings by graph, wheel, or two-axis grid, and assemble a *constellation* of what you are feeling now.

The vocabulary is faithfully Brown's. Her thirteen "Places We Go" — when we compare, when we hurt, when we connect, when the heart is open — are the spine. The selector adds two relational layers: **variations** (semantic siblings: *anguish* near *grief* near *despair*) and **complementaries** (lived co-occurrence: *exhaustion* with *fulfillment*, *foreboding joy* with *gratitude*). Variations are computed from text embeddings of authoritative emotion descriptions. Complementaries are LLM-generated against the eighty-seven and refined by use.

What is missing from the wheel and from the apps is the graph. Feelings are not just close in meaning; they show up together in life. This tool tries to honor both.

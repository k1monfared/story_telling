## When to reach for this

You realize you and a partner haven't done anything specifically with each other (not just co-existing) in weeks. Or transitions between partners feel jarring — there's no marker that says "we're shifting from one configuration to another." Reach for this whenever the relationship needs more visible structure to feel reliable.

## The move

1. Identify what's already a ritual without being named. Sunday morning coffee. The text-good-morning habit. The kiss before leaving. These are the existing practices; name them so they're conscious.
2. Pick one or two new rituals you'd actually keep. Don't over-design. A weekly check-in dinner. A morning hug before phones come out. A monthly date that's protected from cancellation. A pre-departure or post-arrival ritual when one of you has been on a date with another partner.
3. Make them small enough to survive busy weeks. Rituals fail when they're too elaborate; they thrive when they're tiny and reliable.
4. Make them visible. Mark them on the calendar. Talk about them. The partner who reliably knows the next ritual is coming has a different felt sense than the partner trying to remember.
5. Adjust together when life shifts. Rituals can change; what matters is that they remain shared and named, not that they remain identical forever.
6. Especially in nonmonogamy: build rituals around departures and arrivals. A specific way you say goodbye before a date with another partner; a specific way you reconnect after.

## Signal it landed

Your partners can name two or three rituals that anchor the relationship. Hard weeks don't erode the relationship the way they used to because the rituals carry the felt sense of continuity even when energy is low. Transitions between partners feel less like ruptures.

## Failure mode this prevents

Without rituals, the relationship's felt continuity depends on each partner's capacity to actively generate connection in the moment. When capacity drops — sickness, work crunch, mental health periods — the relationship feels uncertain. Rituals let the relationship's structure carry weight that the people can't always carry themselves.

## Reflection prompts

- What routines do you and your partners already have that are meaningful to you and support feeling safe and secure together?
- What rituals or relationship rites of passage have you participated in that brought you closer?
- Are there daily or regular routines that would support you in feeling more secure with your partners?
- What do you and your partners each need to reconnect after being apart? What rituals would help?
- Are there ways of checking in or saying goodbye before a date with another partner that would create more security? How do you want to be approached after?
